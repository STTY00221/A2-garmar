Pronouns & Possessive Forms

Subject Pronouns

Pronoun Example
I I play football.
You You are Australian.
He/She/It He is very tall. / She is a teacher. / It is a dog.
We We love English.
They They have a nice house.

Object Pronouns

Subject Object Example
I me He calls me every day.
you you I like you a lot.
he/she/it him/her/it Give it to him after class.
we us Take us with you.
they them I'll send them an email.

Quantifiers & Determiners

Something/Anything/Nothing

Some- (Positive)

  • somebody/someone
  • something
  • somewhere
There is someone in the room.

Any- (Negative/Question)

  • anybody/anyone
  • anything
  • anywhere
Is there anyone in the room?

No- (Negative)

  • nobody/no-one
  • nothing
  • nowhere
There is no-one in the room.

Much, Many, Little, Few

Countable Nouns

  • many
  • a few
  • few
How many books do you have?

Uncountable Nouns

  • much
  • a little
  • little
How much money do you need?

Too, Too Much/Many, Enough

Form Usage Example
too + adjective More than needed This cake is too sweet.
too much + uncountable Excessive amount You have too much free time.
too many + plural Excessive number There were too many people.
enough + noun Sufficient amount We have enough chairs.
adjective + enough Sufficient degree The room is big enough.

Most, Most of, The Most

Most (Majority)

Most people can't work from home.

Most of + the/this/that/my

Most of the students passed the exam.

The Most (Superlative)

These are the most comfortable sofas.
